Sustainable Development Goal 15:Life On Land

Hero Banner

Target 15.5: PROTECT BIODIVERSITY AND NATURAL HABITATS

Target 15.5 aims to take urgent action to reduce the loss of biodiversity and prevent endangered species from going extinct.

It focuses on protecting natural habitats like forests, wetlands, grasslands, and mountains, which are home to thousands of species.

Why it matters:

  • Biodiversity keeps ecosystems balanced and healthy.

  • Losing species affects food chains, agriculture, and human life.

  • Healthy habitats protect against floods, climate change, and soil erosion.

Examples of actions for Target 15.5:

  • Creating wildlife sanctuaries and national parks

  • Restoring damaged ecosystems

  • Reducing pollution in sensitive areas

  • Reforestation and planting native trees

  • Protecting endangered species such as tigers, rhinos, pandas, etc.

Target 15.7: ELIMINATE POACHING AND TRAFFICKING OF PROTECTED SPECIES

Target 15.7 focuses on stopping poaching, illegal hunting, and the trafficking of protected species.

Many animals are killed for ivory, fur, skins, horns, or sold illegally as exotic pets.

Why it matters:

  • Many animals are killed for ivory, fur, skins, horns, or sold illegally as exotic pets.

  • Illegal wildlife trade is one of the largest criminal industries in the world.

  • Removing animals from ecosystems damages the entire food chain.

  • Wildlife belongs in the wild, not in illegal markets.

Examples of actions for Target 15.7:

  • Stricter laws and penalties for wildlife crime

  • Anti-poaching patrols in forests

  • Tracking and shutting down illegal wildlife markets

  • Technology like drones and camera traps to catch poachers

  • Educating people to avoid buying illegal animal products

Target 15.C: COMBAT GLOBAL POACHING AND TRAFFICKING

Target 15.c is about helping governments and local communities fight poaching and wildlife trafficking.

Many communities living near forests depend on resources for survival and often lack funding or tools to protect wildlife.

Why it matters:

  • Local people are the first line of defense for forests and wildlife.

  • With proper support, communities can protect nature and earn sustainable livelihoods.

  • Cooperation between countries helps stop wildlife smuggling across borders.

Examples of actions for Target 15.C:

  • Providing training and equipment for forest rangers

  • Funding community-based conservation programs

  • Offering alternative jobs so people do not rely on poaching

  • Using technology to monitor wildlife activity

  • International cooperation to stop smuggling networks
