Conserving Forest Ecosystems

Learn how SDG 15 targets protect forests and preserve natural habitats.

Target 15.6: PROMOTE ACCESS TO GENETIC RESOURCES AND FAIR SHARING OF THE BENEFITS

Target 15.6 aims to promote fair and equal sharing of benefits that come from using genetic resources.

Genetic resources include plants, animals, seeds, microorganisms, and natural materials that can be used for medicines, agriculture, cosmetics, and scientific research.

This target supports the idea that countries and communities who provide these natural resources should also receive benefits, such as money, technology, or scientific support.

Why it matters:

  • Many medicines and crops come from plants and organisms found in forests and indigenous lands.

  • Local communities deserve recognition and fair compensation.

  • Prevents exploitation or “biopiracy,” where companies use resources without permission.

  • Encourages research and innovation while respecting nature and local rights.

Card title

Examples of actions for Target 15.6:

  • Agreements that ensure profits are shared with local communities

  • Protecting traditional knowledge about plants

  • Ensuring companies get permission before using natural resources

  • Following international treaties like the Nagoya Protocol

  • Supporting research that benefits both scientists and local people

Target 15.6 aims to ensure that the benefits from using genetic resources—such as plants, animals, seeds, and microorganisms—are shared fairly and responsibly. Many medicines, crops, and products come from natural resources found in forests and indigenous communities. This target helps protect these communities by making sure companies and researchers get permission and share profits or knowledge in return. It also prevents biopiracy and supports sustainable research that respects both nature and local traditions.

Target 15.8: PREVENT INVASIVE ALIEN SPECIES ON LAND AND IN WATER ECOSYSTEMS

Target 15.8 focuses on preventing the introduction and spread of invasive alien species.

Invasive species are plants or animals that come from outside a region and cause harm to the local environment.

They spread quickly, destroy native plants and animals, and upset the balance of ecosystems.

Why it matters:

  • Invasive species threaten biodiversity more than almost any other factor.

  • They can wipe out native species and damage crops and forests.

  • They can cause major economic losses.

  • They spread fast through trade, travel, and climate change.

Card title

Examples of actions for Target 15.6:

  • Blocking the import of harmful species

  • Monitoring borders, ports, and airports

  • Educating the public not to release exotic pets

  • Removing or controlling invasive plants and animals

  • Early detection systems to stop species before they spread

Target 15.8 focuses on preventing harmful invasive species from entering new environments and damaging local ecosystems. Invasive species spread quickly, harm native plants and animals, and can disrupt entire food chains. They often arrive through trade, travel, or accidental introduction. This target encourages countries to monitor borders, raise public awareness, and control or remove these species before they cause serious environmental or economic harm.
